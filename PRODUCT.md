# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local service business owners in the LA area: plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, landscapers, roofers, handymen. Solo operators and small crews (1-5 people). They work with their hands all day and check their phone between jobs. They're missing calls, losing leads to competitors who pick up, and running the whole business out of their head. They're not tech-averse — they use Yelp, Google Business, and text — but they don't have time or patience for complex software. They need something that just works.

## Product Purpose

FlowDesk sells a done-for-you system: professional website + AI phone answering + lead CRM + SMS/email follow-ups + online booking. $97 one-time setup, $197/mo. The product isn't software they configure — it's infrastructure they buy. Success looks like: a contractor who used to miss 7 calls a week now has every call answered, every lead followed up, and a calendar that fills itself.

## Brand Personality

Confident, Direct, No-BS. Talks like a contractor's smart friend who's been through the same pain and found the fix. Doesn't oversell or over-explain. Uses plain language, specific numbers, and real outcomes. The gold-on-dark visual language communicates premium without pretension — earned, not performed.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: Intercom, HubSpot, Typeform vibes. Blue gradients, floating laptop mockups, corporate copy that says "streamline your workflow."
- Agency portfolio: Awwwards bait. Scroll-jacked animations, 12-second load times, art-directed to the point of uselessness.
- Cheap local biz tool: Wix templates, stock photos of handshakes and hardhats, Comic Sans energy, anything that looks like it was built in 2009.

## Design Principles

1. **Say the number.** Vague claims erode trust. Specific numbers ("7 missed calls/week," "$1,500/mo walking out the door") do the work faster than adjectives.
2. **One job at a time.** Every screen has one primary action. Don't ask for attention in three places at once.
3. **Earned, not decorated.** The dark gold palette signals premium because it's restrained — it doesn't shout. Decoration that doesn't carry meaning gets cut.
4. **Talk to the guy in the truck.** Copy is written for someone reading it between jobs on a phone. Short sentences. No jargon. No em dashes.
5. **Show the before.** The pain (missed calls, lost revenue) is always proximate. The product is the contrast, not the hero.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Ensure text contrast on gold-on-dark combinations. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — the audience skews non-tech and may be on older devices.
