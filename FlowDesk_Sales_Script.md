# FlowDesk Sales Script
### Written in the style of Alex Hormozi

---

## THE CORE PREMISE

You are not selling software. You are selling **recovered revenue**.

The business owner is already losing $1,500–$6,000 a month right now, today, before they ever talk to you. Your job is not to convince them to spend money. Your job is to show them they are already spending it — they just aren't getting anything for it.

The frame is not: "Would you like to buy this?"
The frame is: "Do you want to keep bleeding, or do you want to stop?"

---

## PART 1 — COLD OUTREACH (Text or DM)

**Rule:** One sentence of context. One number that hurts. One question. Nothing else.

---

**Version A — Text / iMessage**

> "Hey [Name], saw your HVAC company on Google. Quick question — when you're on a job and miss a call, where does that caller go?"

Wait for reply. Do not pitch yet.

---

**Version B — Facebook / Instagram DM**

> "Hey [Name] — not pitching anything. Genuine question. How many calls a week do you think go unanswered when you're on a job site?"

Wait. If they answer, you have a conversation. If they don't, follow up once in 48 hours:

> "Just the one follow-up — most contractors I talk to say 5–7 missed calls a week. At $250–$300 a job that's $1,500 walking out. Worth a 10-minute call?"

---

**Version C — Cold Email**

> **Subject:** missed calls
>
> [Name] —
>
> You're probably missing 5–7 calls a week when you're on a job.
>
> At $300 average ticket that's $1,500 a week going to whoever answers.
>
> I built a system that answers every call, captures the lead, texts them back automatically, and follows up for 14 days.
>
> It costs $197/month.
>
> Worth 10 minutes?
>
> — Joel

---

## PART 2 — THE OPENER (When They Pick Up)

**Rule:** Never start with a pitch. Start by confirming pain. Let them say it out loud.

---

> "Hey [Name], it's Joel — I sent you a message about the missed call thing. Got two minutes?
>
> Cool. I just want to ask you a few questions, and if it's not a fit I'll tell you straight up.
>
> So — how's the phone situation right now? Like when you're on a job, what happens to calls that come in?"

**Shut up. Let them talk.**

They will say one of three things:

- *"Goes to voicemail"* → "And what percentage of those people actually leave a message?"
- *"My wife handles it / I call back later"* → "Okay, and what's your callback rate? Like realistically, how many of those do you actually reach?"
- *"I miss some, it's fine"* → "Totally. How many would you say a week?"

Keep pulling the thread. Your goal is to get them to say a number.

> "So if it's 5 missed calls a week, and even half of those were real leads — at $300 a job, what does that math look like over a month?"

**Let them do the math.** When they say $2,000–$3,000, that's when you move.

---

## PART 3 — THE PIVOT

Once they've said the number, say this:

> "Yeah. That's the thing. The problem isn't that people aren't calling — they are. The problem is nobody's there to answer, and most people won't leave a voicemail. They just call the next guy on Google.
>
> What I do is I plug that hole. Every call gets answered. Every lead gets captured. And the system follows up automatically for two weeks so you don't have to remember to call anyone back.
>
> Takes about a day to set up. You don't have to do anything technical.
>
> Can I show you what it looks like?"

---

## PART 4 — THE DEMO (10 Minutes, No Fluff)

**Rule:** Show the outcome, not the feature. Every click should answer the question "what does this mean for my business?"

---

**Step 1 — Show the dashboard (30 seconds)**

> "This is what you'd log into every morning. You've got your leads here — name, phone number, what they called about. Every missed call, every text that came in, it's all here. You're not chasing anything."

**Step 2 — Show the lead + follow-up sequence (60 seconds)**

> "When someone calls — let's say it's 9pm, you're off the clock — the system answers for you, captures their info, and immediately sends them a text.
>
> Then it follows up automatically. Day 1, day 3, day 7, day 14. Different messages, not spam. Just — 'Hey, did you still need that quote?' Most of your competitors give up after one callback. You're still in front of that person two weeks later.
>
> This is where the revenue is. It's not the first call. It's the follow-up."

**Step 3 — Show the phone system (60 seconds, or demo live)**

> "The voice system — when someone calls your number, they hear your business name. They can press 1 for emergencies, press 2 to get scheduled. If they press 2, they give their zip code, and a lead shows up in your dashboard in real time.
>
> You want me to call it right now so you can hear it?"

**If yes — call it. Let them hear their business name on a live call. That alone closes half the deals.**

---

## PART 5 — THE PRICE REVEAL

**Rule:** Price comes after value. Never before. And when you say it, don't apologize for it.

---

> "Okay so here's how it works.
>
> It's $197 a month. That includes the AI phone system, the website, the lead dashboard, the automated follow-up — everything. No contracts, cancel any time.
>
> I'll set the whole thing up for you. You don't touch a line of code. It's live within 24–48 hours.
>
> Compare that to a receptionist — that's $3,000 a month, benefits not included, doesn't work nights and weekends, and quits.
>
> You said you're losing around $2,000 a month in missed calls. This costs $197.
>
> The math kind of does itself."

---

## PART 6 — THE CLOSE

Don't ask "what do you think?" That's weak. Assume the close.

> "The easiest way to start is I just need your business name, the phone number you want customers to call, and I'll get it set up. What's the best email to send you the login?"

If they pause:

> "Look — I'm not going to pressure you. But I'll tell you what I tell everyone: the calls are already coming in. The only question is whether you're capturing them or they're going to someone else. You've got nothing to lose on a 30-day trial."

---

## PART 7 — OBJECTION HANDLING

**Rule:** Don't defend. Don't explain. Reverse the frame.

---

**"I need to think about it."**

> "Totally fair. What specifically would you want to think through?"

Wait. They'll give you the real objection. Handle that one.

---

**"It's not the right time."**

> "I get that. When is typically a good time for you — busy season or slow season?"
>
> *(They answer)*
>
> "Here's the thing though — the slower it is, the more every lead matters. This is actually the best time to set it up, because when it gets busy, it's already running and you're not missing anything."

---

**"I already have someone who handles calls."**

> "That's great. Does she work nights and weekends?"
>
> *(No)*
>
> "Right. So this isn't replacing her — it's covering the hours she's not on. Every call that comes in at 8pm or on a Saturday, she doesn't have to deal with. It just shows up in the dashboard for her Monday morning."

---

**"I'm not tech-savvy."**

> "You don't have to be. I set it up. You log in, you see your leads. It's a list of people who called you. That's it."

---

**"I can't afford it right now."**

> "I hear you. Can I ask — if you recovered even one job a month from a missed call, what would that be worth to you?"
>
> *(They answer — usually $300–$500)*
>
> "So one job pays for two months of this. You need one call to go differently to break even. Does that seem reasonable?"

---

**"I tried something like this before and it didn't work."**

> "What was it?"
>
> *(Listen)*
>
> "Yeah — most of those are just lead gen platforms that sell the same lead to five contractors. This is different. It's your number, your system, your leads. Nobody else sees them. The follow-up goes out under your name. It's not a shared marketplace."

---

**"Send me some information."**

> "For sure. I'll send you the link so you can see the actual dashboard. But let me ask — is there anything specific that would make this a yes for you? Because if there is, I'd rather just answer that right now."

---

## PART 8 — THE FOLLOW-UP (If They Don't Close Same Day)

Day 1 (same evening):

> "Hey [Name], sent over the info. Lmk if you have questions — happy to jump on a call."

Day 3:

> "Hey — just checking in. Had another [HVAC / plumbing] guy in [their city] go live this week. He said the first thing that happened was a lead came in at 10pm that he would've missed. Thought of you."

Day 7:

> "Last follow-up from me [Name]. If the timing's off, no worries at all. But if you want to get it set up before the weekend, I can have it live by Friday. Just say the word."

**After that — move on.** Chasing is for people with bad offers.
